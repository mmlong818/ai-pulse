Mistral lands Microsoft pact as Samsung weighs €1B investment

The French lab expanded its Microsoft partnership to build sovereign European AI infrastructure, while Samsung reportedly weighs up to €1B at a €20B valuation.

Mistral AI had a defining 48 hours: on July 21 it announced an expanded global strategic partnership with Microsoft to build AI infrastructure across Europe, and by July 22 the Financial Times reported Samsung is in talks to invest up to €1 billion in the French lab at a valuation of roughly €20 billion.

The Microsoft deal

The multi-billion-dollar partnership centers on European compute: infrastructure built around thousands of Nvidia's Vera Rubin GPUs, complementing the €1.2 billion Mistral has already committed to data centers in Sweden. Mistral's models — including Medium 3.5 and OCR 4 — will be integrated into Microsoft Foundry and Copilot Studio, with deployment options spanning Azure, Azure Local, on-premises, and fully offline environments. Both companies are pitching the same message: Microsoft president Brad Smith framed it as giving Europe powerful AI without surrendering control of its data, while CEO Arthur Mensch cast Mistral as the sovereignty-friendly frontier vendor for regulated industries like finance, healthcare, and manufacturing.

The Samsung round

The reported Samsung investment, with Swedish investor EQT also said to be participating, would lift Mistral's valuation to about €20 billion — up from €12 billion less than a year ago. Samsung, which first backed Mistral through its venture arm in 2024, is deepening AI exposure across the stack; it is separately negotiating with Anthropic over custom AI chip manufacturing. Mistral has passed a $400 million revenue run rate, and Mensch has projected annual revenue exceeding $1 billion by year-end.

Why it matters

Europe's flagship AI lab is executing a distinctive playbook: rather than racing US labs on raw frontier capability, Mistral is monetizing data sovereignty — and the twin announcements show both demand sides converging, with the world's largest software distributor selling its models into regulated enterprises and Asia's hardware giants buying strategic stakes. If the round closes at €20 billion, Mistral cements its position as the value benchmark for AI champions outside the US-China axis.
