White House, top AI labs near voluntary 30-day model review deal

OpenAI, Anthropic and Google are close to a voluntary pact giving US agencies up to 30 days to vet frontier models before release, expected by August 1.

The White House is finalizing a voluntary framework with OpenAI, Anthropic and Google that would give federal agencies up to 30 days to review new frontier AI models for national-security risks before they reach the public, according to multiple reports published July 21. An announcement is expected before August 1.

Built on the June executive order

The deal would operationalize an executive order signed on June 2, which created the first federal framework specifically governing how frontier models reach the market. That order established the 30-day government preview window, a classified benchmarking process run by the NSA to flag models with advanced cyber capabilities, and a voluntary AI cybersecurity clearinghouse. Notably, the order explicitly prohibits agencies from treating the arrangement as a mandatory licensing or preclearance regime. Meta is reportedly not part of the negotiations, leaving the pact concentrated on the three labs generally regarded as operating at the frontier.

Voluntary on paper, weighty in practice

Reporting around the framework suggests the government's informal leverage is already substantial. Export-control authority was reportedly used earlier this year to take Anthropic's flagship models offline for 18 days, and the White House is said to have asked OpenAI to delay the full public rollout of its top GPT-5.6 tier. For the labs, signing on buys predictability and a formal channel with the administration; for the government, it secures early access without legislation.

The significance is that the United States is converging on a de facto pre-release gate for frontier AI without any act of Congress. The voluntary structure sidesteps legal challenges but raises hard questions: obligations enforced through relationships and export leverage rather than law, and a perimeter that excludes Meta, open-weight developers and Chinese labs entirely. With formal US-China AI talks scheduled for September, the framework will effectively define the American governance posture that other jurisdictions — and rivals — respond to.
