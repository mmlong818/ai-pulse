Ant International raises $1.2 billion to push agentic commerce

The Singapore-based fintech raised about $1.2 billion in Series A funding from Ant Group, Alibaba and others to expand AI-driven payments.

Ant International, the Singapore-headquartered global arm spun out of China's Ant Group, has raised approximately $1.2 billion in Series A equity financing to expand its cross-border payments business and build out AI-driven "agentic commerce" services. Existing shareholders Ant Group and Alibaba Group participated in the round announced this week, alongside international institutional investors.

Betting on AI agents that shop and pay

The company said proceeds will fund merchant payments, treasury management, credit and account services for businesses ranging from small merchants to large enterprises. A central theme is agentic commerce: AI systems that handle multistep commercial workflows — discovering products, comparing options, completing purchases and settling payments — on a user's behalf. Ant International this year introduced an Agentic Mobile Protocol (AMP), a standard intended to let merchants, digital wallets, LLM platforms and agent builders plug payment capabilities into AI agents without rebuilding their existing systems.

Ant International has operated independently of Ant Group since 2024, with principal markets across Asia, Europe, the Middle East and Latin America. Its infrastructure reportedly serves on the order of 150 million merchants and connects to roughly two billion user accounts through wallet partnerships, giving it one of the larger distribution footprints in global payments outside the card networks.

Why it matters

The raise is one of the clearest signals yet that the payments industry expects AI agents to become genuine economic actors, and that the plumbing for agent-initiated transactions is now a venture-scale race. Visa, Mastercard, Stripe and PayPal have all launched agent-payment initiatives this year, and a well-capitalized challenger with deep Asian merchant networks raises the competitive stakes — particularly in emerging markets where wallet-based payments dominate. The round also shows the Alibaba-Ant ecosystem continuing its international expansion through structures insulated from the domestic Chinese entity, a pattern shaped by Beijing's earlier fintech crackdown. If agentic commerce grows as its backers expect, the protocols being seeded now will determine who collects the tolls when software, rather than people, does the buying.
