Alibaba previews 2.4-trillion-parameter Qwen3.8-Max model

Alibaba's 2.4-trillion-parameter multimodal flagship debuted in preview during WAIC, with the Qwen team pledging an open-weight release to follow.

Alibaba's Qwen team used the World AI Conference in Shanghai to preview Qwen3.8-Max, a mixture-of-experts model with roughly 2.4 trillion parameters that the company describes as its most capable system yet. The July 19 announcement came just days after rival Moonshot AI launched Kimi K3, underlining the accelerating release cadence among China's leading laboratories.

First trillion-scale multimodal Qwen

Qwen3.8-Max is the first Qwen model above the trillion-parameter mark to accept multimodal input, handling text, images, video and document understanding in a single system. Alibaba says the preview beats its predecessor, Qwen3.7-Max, on coding, full-stack development, data analysis and office workflows. In a post on X, the team went further and called the model "second only to Fable 5," referring to Anthropic's flagship — a bold claim that independent benchmarks have yet to confirm.

The preview is live on Alibaba's developer platforms at about a tenth of standard pricing, a discounting tactic the company has used before to seed adoption ahead of general availability.

Open weights promised

Alibaba says open weights will follow, though it has not committed to a date. Publication at this scale would place Qwen3.8-Max among the largest open models ever released, alongside Moonshot's 2.8-trillion-parameter Kimi K3, whose weights are due out on July 27, and ahead of DeepSeek's V4 stable release expected later this week.

Why it matters

China's top labs are now shipping frontier-scale models weeks apart and increasingly pairing them with open-weight commitments — a strategy that spreads their architectures through the global developer ecosystem while offloading inference costs that domestic chip supplies cannot absorb. For enterprise buyers, a credible near-frontier model at a tenth of typical pricing intensifies downward pressure on API prices industry-wide. For Washington, the sight of Chinese firms fielding systems above two trillion parameters despite years of chip export controls will feed an already contentious debate about whether the restrictions are slowing China's frontier progress at all.
