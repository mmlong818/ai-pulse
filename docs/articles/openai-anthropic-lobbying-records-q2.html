OpenAI and Anthropic set quarterly lobbying records in Q2 2026

Federal filings show OpenAI and Anthropic spent a record $3.17 million combined on Q2 lobbying, as Washington drafts the rules for frontier AI.

OpenAI and Anthropic spent a combined $3.17 million on federal lobbying in the second quarter of 2026, a 23 percent increase over the first quarter and a quarterly record for both companies, according to disclosures reported July 21.

The numbers

Anthropic spent $1.97 million between April and June, up 26 percent from the prior quarter, bringing its first-half total above $3.5 million — already more than the $3.1 million it spent in all of 2025. OpenAI spent $1.2 million, up roughly 18 percent. Meta remained the largest spender among AI-exposed companies at $5.99 million, though its outlay fell 15 percent, and CNBC noted that legacy tech and defense lobbying budgets are broadly flat or declining even as the frontier labs accelerate. Filings show the two labs concentrated on export controls, cybersecurity, AI safety standards, copyright, cloud computing and defense contracts.

Why the money is moving now

The spending surge tracks the highest-stakes policy stretch the industry has faced. The White House is finalizing its voluntary 30-day frontier-model review framework, export-control authority has reportedly been used against Anthropic's own flagship models this year, formal US-China AI talks are set for September, and political groups aligned with both labs are already spending heavily ahead of the midterms.

The disclosures matter as a milestone in the AI industry's political maturation. In absolute terms the labs remain small players — Meta alone outspends them threefold — but the growth rate tells the real story: Anthropic's lobbying budget has roughly doubled year over year at a moment when review windows, export rules and copyright liability are being written into durable policy. The companies that shape those rules now will be living with them, and profiting or suffering from them, for the rest of the decade.
