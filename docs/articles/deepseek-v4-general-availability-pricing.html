DeepSeek V4 hits general availability with peak-hour API pricing

DeepSeek's V4 models reached general availability with million-token context and China's first peak-and-off-peak API pricing, undercutting Western rivals.

DeepSeek moved its V4 model family from preview to general availability on July 19, roughly three months after its initial release, in Pro and Flash variants with a one-million-token context window. Legacy endpoints are scheduled to be switched over on July 24, giving developers only days to migrate.

Grid economics come to inference

The most consequential change is commercial rather than technical: DeepSeek has introduced peak-and-valley pricing, a first for a major model API. During peak blocks — 9:00 a.m. to noon and 2:00 to 6:00 p.m. Beijing time — usage is billed at double the off-peak rate. Off-peak, V4 Pro costs roughly $0.87 per million output tokens and the lightweight V4 Flash about $0.28, with steep discounts for cached inputs. Early testers report that V4 Pro approaches the performance of Anthropic's Claude Opus 4.8 and OpenAI's GPT-5.6 Sol on common workloads at a small fraction of their prices.

A crowded Chinese release cycle

The general-availability push lands in the middle of an intense stretch for Chinese AI: Moonshot's Kimi K3 recently halted new sign-ups after demand outran its GPU supply, and Alibaba's Qwen team has been previewing its next flagship. Chinese open-weight and low-cost models are increasingly functioning as the global price anchor against which Western closed models are judged.

The pricing structure is the real story. Time-of-day billing imports electricity-grid economics into AI inference and is a frank admission that GPU capacity, not model quality, is the binding constraint on Chinese providers. For Western labs, near-frontier performance at commodity prices compresses margins on mainstream workloads and pushes differentiation toward the very top of the capability range. For developers worldwide — particularly outside the US and Europe — V4's numbers make a Chinese model the rational default for cost-sensitive applications, deepening the bifurcation of the global AI stack that policymakers on both sides are already grappling with.
