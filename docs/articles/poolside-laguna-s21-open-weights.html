Poolside's Laguna S 2.1 tops SWE-Bench Multilingual at 8B active

Poolside's 118B-parameter MoE coder, released under a permissive open license, tops SWE-Bench Multilingual while activating just 8B parameters per token.

Poolside released Laguna S 2.1 on July 21, an open-weight agentic coding model the company bills as the most capable open-weight model built in the West. The weights are available on Hugging Face under the permissive OpenMDW-1.1 license.

Small activation, big scores

Laguna S 2.1 is a mixture-of-experts model with 118 billion total parameters but only 8 billion active per token, paired with a 1-million-token context window. On SWE-Bench Multilingual it scores 78.5%, topping the published leaderboard outright; on the public split of SWE-Bench Pro it posts 59.4%, and on Terminal-Bench 2.1 it reaches 70.2% — first among open models of disclosed size on Poolside's compiled table, behind only larger or closed systems.

The training story is as notable as the scores: Poolside says the model went from the start of pre-training on May 22 to public launch in under nine weeks, on a comparatively modest fleet of 4,096 Nvidia H200 GPUs. The small active-parameter count means the model can run on a single high-end desktop, putting frontier-adjacent coding capability within reach of individual developers and on-prem deployments.

The geopolitical subtext

The release lands in the middle of a heated debate — visible from Washington policy circles to weekend commentary — about Chinese open-weight models like Qwen, DeepSeek and Kimi dominating the open ecosystem while US labs keep their best models closed. Poolside is explicitly claiming that ground: its announcement leans on the phrase 'the West's most capable open-weight model' rather than any absolute crown.

Why it matters

If the benchmark numbers hold up under independent testing, Laguna S 2.1 shifts two curves at once: what open weights can do in agentic coding, and how cheaply a competitive model can be trained. For enterprises weighing self-hosted coding agents against API dependencies — and for policymakers fretting that the open-model race has been ceded to China — a permissively licensed Western model at this level is a meaningful data point.
